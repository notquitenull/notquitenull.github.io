Correlating Donald Trump's Tweets with Polymarket Price Movement

This project was developed as part of the course "Computational Methods for Open Source Intelligence" at Ruhr University Bochum. It explores the potential impact of Donald Trump's social media posts on prediction markets using publicly available data.

In particular, we combined social media data with historical Polymarket data to investigate whether Donald Trump's public statements are associated with changes in prediction market prices.

Problem Formulation

Motivation

Donald Trump, the 45ᵗʰ and 47ᵗʰ President of the United States of America, is known for his extensive activity on social media platforms such as Twitter and later Truth Social. His posts often address real-world events and frequently receive significant public attention, as they are highly controversial. As one of the most influential political figures in the world, his communication reaches millions of people almost instantly, potentially influencing public opinion and reactions to current events.

Prediction markets reflect participants' expectations about future events. However, participants' beliefs may be influenced by new public information. As Polymarket is one of the most profitable prediction markets in the world, we chose to analyse the influence of Trump's social media posts on polymarket.

In fact, Polymarket hosts thousands of active prediction markets. Each market contains a question about some real-world event, where participants can trade on the likelihood of either a "Yes" or "No" outcome. Since these probabilities are continuously updated based on participants' expectations, Polymarket provides a way to analyse how drastically major public events or statements, such as Trump's social media posts, may influence the expectations and beliefs of people.

The potential influence of a single public figure like Donald Trump on prediction markets raises important questions about the relationship between political communication and financial decisions. If statements from Donald Trump can significantly affect market expectations, this could indicate that in general his posts have a measurable impact on collective beliefs and people's decisions. This is particularly relevant given the controversial nature of many public statements made by Trump and raises the question of how strongly market participants rely on information from such sources when making financial decisions. Furthermore, this emphasises the magnitude of him being banned from social media platforms. Thus, analysing the relationship between Trump's social media activity and Polymarket movements is of high significance.

Research Question

To address this within a reasonable scope, we came up with the following research question:

"To what extent are Donald Trump's social media posts correlated with price movements on Polymarket?"

Ethics

For ethics, we considered the following aspects:
  • We use and scrape publicly available posts from Donald Trump. Since Donald Trump is a public figure and these posts represent his public statements, we consider their use to raise no significant privacy concerns.
  • The Polymarket dataset we are using contains user data, in the form of wallet IDs attached to transactions. The wallet IDs contain no direct personal data but can be used to query for the corresponding user profile on the Polymarket API. Our analysis drops the wallet IDs as they are not relevant to the pricing trend. All information used is publicly available on the Polygon blockchain.
  • The results of this project should NOT be interpreted as evidence of a causal relationship between Trump's posts and polymarket movements, as both the posts and the market movements may be influenced by other factors.
  • The results of our analysis are intended for research purposes only and should not be used as the basis for betting decisions on Polymarket or other markets.
